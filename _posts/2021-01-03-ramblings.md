write short paragraphs of what I'm thinking. It's like a diary, but worse.

#### 1/3/2021 Gun Point

A few years ago, I had an acquaintance tell me, "I don't understand why people in movies wet their pants 
when held at gun point. I don't think I'd be scared even if someone pointed their gun at me." At the time, 
I thought, _wow, what a stupid thought_. Even now, I still think that was a stupid thought. It probably 
highlights a problem with [desensitization towards violence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desensitization_(psychology)#Effects_on_violence) 
or something of that sort. But what I'm more curious about is how this person would react if a close one passed away.
It's one thing to not fear a gun, but to disregard your own life probably means they don't understand 
the fragility of their own lives and the lives around them.
